Playgroup Learning Track

Age Limit: 1.5 - 2.5 Years

A playgroup curriculum focuses on tactile experiences, basic motor development, speaking, and social adaptability. The classroom is free-flowing and filled with safe foam blocks and toys.

Expected Learning Outcomes

  • Develop gross motor skills (crawling, basic balancing, holding small wooden pegs).
  • Improve verbal abilities through sing-alongs, rhymes, and interactive actions.
  • Social adjustments (sharing toys, greeting teachers, sitting in child groups).
  • Basic toilet routines and independent cleanup instructions.

Nursery Learning Track

Age Limit: 2.5 - 3.5 Years

Nursery introduces interactive art-based education, pre-writing pencil holding, color/shape recognition, and dramatic role-plays to build emotional independence.

Expected Learning Outcomes

  • Identify colors, geometric shapes, and group objects by categories.
  • Phonics sounds for letters A to Z and simple word identification.
  • Independent painting, clay sculpting, and block coloring skills.
  • Express complex emotions clearly and ask logical questions to peers.

Junior KG Learning Track

Age Limit: 3.5 - 4.5 Years

Junior KG features systematic writing drills, reading basic three-letter words, simple math additions using visual grids, and engaging early environmental science lessons.

Expected Learning Outcomes

  • Write uppercase and lowercase letters neatly on grids.
  • Basic counting up to 50 and simple additions under 10.
  • Understand local nature concepts (seasons, animals, plant structures).
  • Interact fluidly in conversational English and participate in speech.

Senior KG Learning Track

Age Limit: 4.5 - 5.5 Years

Preparing kids for primary school. Focuses on descriptive reading, math additions/subtractions, creative science hypotheses, and building leadership and team behaviors.

Expected Learning Outcomes

  • Simple reading of 4-5 line storybooks and early environmental rules.
  • Perform visual subtraction and list numbers up to 100 correctly.
  • Lead classroom tasks, host circle discussions, and share.
  • Practice basic computer mouse skills and digital screen puzzles.
